# Copyright Infringement and Takedown Policy

**Version:** 1.0  
**Last updated:** 2026-05-28  
**Jurisdiction:** India (primary); United States DMCA (secondary — for US rights-holders)  
**Governing law:** Information Technology Act 2000 §79; IT (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules 2021; Copyright Act 1957 (India); Digital Millennium Copyright Act 1998 (US, honoured voluntarily)

---

## 1. Shoprift's Position as Intermediary

**1.1** Shoprift operates as a technology intermediary. It processes store data — product descriptions, images, and metadata — extracted from a Source Platform at a Seller's instruction and delivers it to the Seller.

**1.2** Shoprift does not create, publish, or host Content independently. Content is provided by Sellers who represent and warrant in the Migration Consent & Authorisation Agreement that they own or have the right to extract and use it.

**1.3** Shoprift qualifies for safe-harbour protection under IT Act §79 provided it acts expeditiously to remove or disable access to infringing content upon receiving actual knowledge of the infringement. This policy is how Shoprift operationalises that obligation.

**1.4** Shoprift also voluntarily complies with the notice-and-takedown procedures of the US Digital Millennium Copyright Act (17 U.S.C. §512) for the benefit of US-based rights-holders, without conceding that US law applies.

---

## 2. What This Policy Covers

**2.1** This policy applies to claims that:
- A Delivery Package produced by Shoprift contains content that infringes the copyright of a third party
- Shoprift's Service was used to extract content to which the Seller did not have extraction rights
- Shoprift's own marketing or product materials infringe a third party's copyright

**2.2** This policy does not apply to:
- Trade mark infringement (contact grievance officer directly)
- Patent infringement (contact grievance officer directly)
- Defamation or privacy claims (see Terms of Service §7)
- Disputes between a Seller and the Source Platform about their mutual contractual obligations

## 3. Submitting a Copyright Infringement Notice

**3.1** If you believe that content processed or delivered through Shoprift infringes your copyright, submit a written notice to the Designated Copyright Agent:

> **Designated Copyright Agent / Grievance Officer**  
> Name: Mayank Malik  
> Email: 001henrycreel@gmail.com  
> Subject line: COPYRIGHT INFRINGEMENT NOTICE — [brief description]

**3.2** Your notice must include all of the following to be valid and actionable:

1. **Identification of the work** — describe the copyrighted work claimed to have been infringed, with sufficient detail for Shoprift to locate it (e.g. original publication URL, registration number if applicable).

2. **Identification of the infringing material** — identify the specific content in Shoprift's system that you claim infringes your work. Include any job reference number or Delivery Package details if known.

3. **Your contact information** — full legal name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address.

4. **Statement of good faith belief** — a statement that you have a good faith belief that use of the material in the manner complained of is not authorised by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.

5. **Statement of accuracy** — a statement, under penalty of perjury (if submitting under US law) or declaration of truth (if submitting under Indian law), that the information in your notice is accurate and that you are the copyright owner or are authorised to act on the copyright owner's behalf.

6. **Signature** — a physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or authorised representative.

**3.3** Notices that are materially incomplete will not be acted upon. Shoprift will notify the sender of any deficiencies within 5 business days.

## 4. Shoprift's Response to Valid Notices

**4.1 Acknowledgement:** Shoprift will acknowledge receipt of a valid notice within 24 hours.

**4.2 Review:** Shoprift will review the notice within 3 business days of receipt.

**4.3 Action options — Shoprift will take one of the following:**

- **Remove or disable access** to the infringing content in the Delivery Package if Shoprift has access and control over the relevant copy
- **Notify the Seller** that a claim has been received relating to their job and request they not publish or use the disputed content pending resolution
- **Decline to act** where the notice is clearly deficient, the claim is facially implausible, or the content is clearly lawful

**4.4** Shoprift will notify the affected Seller of any takedown action, unless doing so would compromise a law enforcement investigation or court order.

**4.5** Shoprift does not adjudicate disputes between a rights-holder and a Seller. If the parties dispute ownership of the content, Shoprift will maintain its takedown pending a court order or written resolution signed by both parties.

## 5. Counter-Notice Procedure

**5.1** If a Seller believes their content was removed as a result of a mistaken or misidentified claim, they may submit a counter-notice to the Designated Copyright Agent at the contact above.

**5.2** A valid counter-notice must include:

1. **Identification of the removed content** — sufficient description for Shoprift to identify the specific content taken down.

2. **Statement of good faith belief** — a statement that you have a good faith belief that the content was removed as a result of mistake or misidentification.

3. **Consent to jurisdiction** — a statement consenting to jurisdiction of competent Indian courts (and, if applicable, the US federal court for your district) for any dispute with the complaining party.

4. **Your contact information** — full legal name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address.

5. **Signature** — physical or electronic signature.

**5.3** Upon receipt of a valid counter-notice, Shoprift will:
- Forward the counter-notice to the original complaining party
- Wait 10 business days
- Restore access to the content unless the complaining party notifies Shoprift that they have filed a court action seeking to restrain the Seller

## 6. Repeat Infringers

**6.1** Shoprift maintains a record of valid infringement notices. Sellers against whom three or more valid notices are received within any 12-month period will have their accounts terminated.

**6.2** Shoprift reserves the right to terminate accounts of Sellers who are found to have submitted fraudulent or bad-faith counter-notices.

---

## 7. Abuse of This Process

**7.1** Submitting a knowingly false infringement notice is a serious matter. Under US law (17 U.S.C. §512(f)), a complaining party who knowingly misrepresents that content is infringing may be liable for damages. Under Indian law, false notices may constitute fraud or abuse of process.

**7.2** Shoprift reserves the right to pursue legal remedies against parties who submit fraudulent takedown notices targeting legitimate Seller content.
