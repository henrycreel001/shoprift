## 1. Overview

Shoprift provides a one-time data migration service. A single payment covers a single extraction job for a single store. This policy explains when and how refunds are issued.

This policy is part of the Agreements between the Seller and Shoprift. Defined terms have the same meaning as in the Terms of Service.

## 2. Before Extraction Begins

**2.1 Cancellation before job starts.**  
If the Seller's extraction job has not yet started at the time of cancellation request, a full refund will be issued. No questions asked.

**2.2 How to cancel before extraction begins.**  
Email 001henrycreel@gmail.com with the subject line: `Cancellation — [your store URL]`. Include your job ID if available.

**2.3 What "not yet started" means.**  
The extraction job is considered started when the Shoprift worker begins connecting to the Source Platform. For self-serve web app jobs, this typically occurs within minutes of payment confirmation. If you are unsure, email within 30 minutes of payment and we will check.

## 3. After Extraction Begins

Once the extraction job starts, the refund terms depend on the outcome:

**3.1 Complete failure.**  
If the extraction produces zero products (the worker could not connect to the Source Platform, or no products were extracted at all), a **full refund** will be issued automatically. You do not need to request it.

**3.2 Significant partial failure.**  
If the extraction produces fewer than 70% of the products estimated in the pre-payment preview:
- Shoprift will first offer a free re-run. If the re-run brings the count to 70% or above, no refund is issued.
- If the re-run also falls below 70%, a **partial refund** will be issued proportional to the shortfall, at Shoprift's reasonable discretion.
- Example: Preview estimated 100 products. Extraction delivered 50 and re-run delivered 55. Refund = approximately 45% of the amount paid.

**3.3 Successful delivery.**  
If the extraction delivers 70% or more of the estimated products and the Delivery Package (JSON, CSV, images) is made available for download, the service has been rendered and no refund is available.

**3.4 No refund for Source Platform data quality.**  
Shoprift extracts what is present on the Source Platform at the time of extraction. If products have missing images, incomplete descriptions, or incorrect prices on the Source Platform, this is not a Shoprift failure. No refund is issued for data quality issues that originate with the Source Platform.

**3.5 No refund for import compatibility issues.**  
The Delivery Package is formatted according to Shoprift's published Shopify import format. If the Seller is unable to import the package into their chosen target platform for reasons unrelated to data completeness, this is not grounds for a refund.

## 4. How to Request a Refund

**4.1** Email 001henrycreel@gmail.com with the subject line: `Refund Request — [your store URL]`

**4.2** Include:
- Your store URL
- Your job ID (shown on the confirmation screen or in the email you received)
- A brief description of the issue

**4.3** Refund requests must be submitted within **7 days** of the extraction job date. Requests submitted after this window may not be honoured.

**4.4** Shoprift will acknowledge your request within 24 hours and resolve it within 15 days, in accordance with the IT Rules 2021.

---

## 5. Refund Method and Timeline

**5.1 Self-serve web app payments (Shopify Billing).**  
Refunds for payments made through the Shoprift Shopify app are issued through Shopify's billing system. The refund will appear against the Seller's Shopify account within **7–10 business days** of approval. The exact timing depends on Shopify's billing cycle.

**5.2 Concierge mode payments (UPI / Razorpay).**  
Refunds for direct payments are issued to the original payment method via the same channel (UPI reference, or Razorpay reversal). Timeline: **5–7 business days** of approval.

**5.3** Shoprift does not issue refunds in cash or via any method other than the original payment channel.

## 6. Exceptions — When No Refund Is Issued

No refund is issued in the following situations:

**6.1** The Seller provided an incorrect store URL, causing the extraction to run on the wrong store.

**6.2** The Seller's store was taken down, made private, or became inaccessible between the recon preview and the extraction run. Shoprift is not liable for changes to the Source Platform after payment.

**6.3** The Seller's ownership verification failed or was revoked before extraction completed.

**6.4** The extraction succeeded and the Delivery Package was downloaded or accessed by the Seller.

**6.5** The refund request was submitted more than 7 days after the extraction job date.
