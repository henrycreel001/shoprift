# Acceptable Use Policy

**Version:** 1.0  
**Last updated:** 2026-05-28  
**Jurisdiction:** India  
**Governing law:** Information Technology Act 2000; Information Technology (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules 2021; Consumer Protection Act 2019; Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023

---

## 1. Purpose

**1.1** This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") governs what Sellers may and may not do when using the Shoprift service ("Service"). It supplements and is incorporated into the Terms of Service.

**1.2** Shoprift operates the Service as an intermediary under the Information Technology Act 2000 ("IT Act"). Compliance with this AUP is a condition of Shoprift maintaining safe-harbour protection under IT Act §79 and IT Rules 2021. Sellers who breach this AUP expose themselves to direct liability and may be reported to relevant authorities.

**1.3** Capitalised terms not defined here have the meanings given in the Terms of Service.

---

## 2. Permitted Use

**2.1** The Service is provided solely for the purpose of migrating a Seller's own store data from a Source Platform to a Target Platform.

**2.2** Sellers may use the Service only for stores they own or operate and for which they have the authority to authorise extraction, as confirmed in the Migration Consent & Authorisation Agreement.

**2.3** Sellers may share the Delivery Package with service providers (e.g. developers, agencies) assisting with their migration, provided those parties are bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations.

---

## 3. Prohibited Content

Sellers must not use the Service to migrate, process, or import content that:

**3.1 Illegal goods or services**
- Controlled substances, narcotics, or precursor chemicals not lawfully sold by the Seller
- Weapons, ammunition, or explosives requiring a licence the Seller does not hold
- Counterfeit goods, pirated software, or products infringing intellectual property rights
- Wildlife products protected under the Wildlife (Protection) Act 1972 or CITES
- Products subject to international trade sanctions applicable to India

**3.2 Harmful or deceptive content**
- Content that is obscene under Indian Penal Code §292 or the IT Act §67
- Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) — any instance will be reported immediately to law enforcement and CyberTipline (NCMEC)
- Content designed to deceive consumers about the nature, origin, quality, or price of products
- Fraudulent, misleading, or deceptive product listings

**3.3 Third-party rights violations**
- Content the Seller does not have the right to extract, reproduce, or publish
- Personally identifiable information of third parties extracted without lawful basis under the DPDP Act 2023
- Customer order data, payment data, or contact details of the Source Platform's buyers, unless the Seller has the legal authority and appropriate consent from those individuals

**3.4 Regulated categories**
- Pharmaceutical products, medical devices, or health supplements requiring regulatory approval without disclosing required approvals
- Financial products or investment schemes requiring SEBI or RBI registration
- Tobacco, alcohol, or gambling products in any Indian state where such products require special licences

---

## 4. Prohibited Conduct

Sellers must not:

**4.1** Use the Service to extract data from a store they do not own or operate, regardless of any claimed permission from the store's owner.

**4.2** Attempt to circumvent Shoprift's verification mechanism or provide false or misleading information during the ownership verification process.

**4.3** Use the Service in a manner that violates the Source Platform's terms of service where the Seller has agreed to those terms — Shoprift does not advise on this and accepts no liability; compliance is the Seller's sole responsibility.

**4.4** Use automated scripts, bots, or tools to submit multiple simultaneous or sequential jobs in order to artificially increase extraction volume or circumvent per-job pricing.

**4.5** Resell, sublicense, or white-label access to the Service without Shoprift's prior written consent.

**4.6** Reverse engineer, decompile, or attempt to extract source code from any part of the Service.

**4.7** Interfere with, disrupt, or conduct denial-of-service attacks against the Service or any infrastructure it relies upon.

**4.8** Use the Service for competitive intelligence gathering, price scraping, or market research beyond migrating the Seller's own store.

**4.9** Misrepresent their identity, business, or ownership of a store to obtain access to the Service or to obtain a refund.

**4.10** Provide false information to Shoprift in connection with any complaint, dispute, or legal notice.

## 5. Third-Party Data and DPDP Act Obligations

**5.1** If a Seller's store data incidentally includes personal data of their customers (names, email addresses, phone numbers, order history), the Seller is the Data Fiduciary under the DPDP Act 2023 and bears full responsibility for:
- Having a lawful basis for processing that data
- Having appropriate consents from data principals
- Ensuring the Target Platform handles the data lawfully

**5.2** Shoprift processes any such incidental personal data solely as a Data Processor acting on the Seller's instructions. The Data Processing Agreement governs this relationship.

**5.3** Sellers must not use the Service if they do not have a lawful basis to migrate their customer data. If in doubt, the Seller should exclude customer data from the migration scope and contact Shoprift for guidance.

## 6. Enforcement

**6.1** Shoprift may, without notice, suspend or terminate a Seller's access to the Service if Shoprift has reasonable grounds to believe the Seller has violated this AUP.

**6.2** Shoprift will not issue refunds for jobs terminated due to AUP violations.

**6.3** Shoprift may report violations involving illegal content or conduct to:
- Relevant law enforcement agencies in India
- The Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in)
- The National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (1930)
- Any other competent authority

**6.4** Shoprift cooperates with lawful requests from Indian courts and government agencies. Sellers have no expectation of confidentiality in respect of data disclosed pursuant to a valid court order or government requisition.

**6.5** A decision to enforce or not enforce this AUP in one instance does not waive Shoprift's right to enforce it in any future instance.
