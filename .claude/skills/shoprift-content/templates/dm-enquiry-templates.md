# DM & Enquiry Templates

Patterns for inbound DMs and enquiries on @shoprift, @mayankmalikx, and email. **Never copy-paste these verbatim** — personalize the first line every time. These are shape guides, not scripts.

**Sign-off**: "— Mayank" on personal replies. @shoprift account: no sign-off (it's the brand voice).

---

## Common scenarios

### 1. "How does this work?" (curious browser, not a buyer yet)

**Shape**: Answer in 3 lines. Offer a 2-minute walkthrough.

> Hey [first name],
>
> Short version: send me your dm2buy URL, I extract every product/image/collection, and you get a CSV ready to import into Shopify. About 3 minutes once I have access to scan it.
>
> Want me to do a quick scan first so you can see what comes out?
>
> — Mayank

---

### 2. "How much does it cost?" (before pricing is public)

**Shape**: Don't apologize. Be direct. Convert to a free scan.

> Custom quote per store. The number depends on product count and how complex the catalog is.
>
> Send me the dm2buy URL and I'll do a free scan — you'll see exactly what you'd get back, and I'll quote off that.
>
> — Mayank

---

### 3. "Will I lose [data field]?"

**Shape**: Direct yes/no. Then the detail.

> No. Every product field carries over — title, price, description, images, variants, SKUs, collections.
>
> Where stuff sometimes drops out: variants on dm2buy that don't have prices set (rare), and descriptions that are just shipping boilerplate (those I usually skip on purpose — your call).
>
> Send the URL if you want a sample of what your output would look like.
>
> — Mayank

---

### 4. "What if my migration breaks something?"

**Shape**: Acknowledge real, explain the safety, offer the proof.

> Fair question.
>
> The migration outputs a CSV — your dm2buy store stays untouched. Nothing on your live store changes. You import the CSV into Shopify whenever you're ready, and if something looks off, you delete the products and try again.
>
> I also send a migration report with every export — every product accounted for, every image checked. If anything's missing, you'll know.
>
> — Mayank

---

### 5. "When is the tool live?"

**Shape**: Honest. Offer the alternative.

> Building it now — no fixed date yet, but soon.
>
> In the meantime, I'm taking on a handful of manual migrations per week. Same output, same CSV, just done by me. If you want one of those slots, send the dm2buy URL.
>
> — Mayank

---

### 6. "Can you do [non-dm2buy platform]?"

**Shape**: Honest about current scope. Note the future.

> Today: just dm2buy → Shopify (and any platform that takes a CSV import — WooCommerce, BigCommerce, Wix).
>
> Other source platforms (Shopify, Wix, Squarespace as the *source*) are on the roadmap but not built yet. What's your current setup?
>
> — Mayank

---

### 7. The "migrate" trigger word (someone DM'd just "migrate" or similar from a campaign)

**Shape**: Welcoming. Get the URL fast. No fluff.

> Hey — thanks for reaching out.
>
> Drop your dm2buy URL here and I'll run a free scan. You'll see how many products, images, and collections come out before we talk about anything else.
>
> — Mayank

---

### 8. Long, detailed enquiry with multiple questions

**Shape**: Number the answers. Match their effort.

> Hey [name],
>
> Quick answers in order:
>
> 1. [answer to Q1]
> 2. [answer to Q2]
> 3. [answer to Q3]
>
> Want to send the dm2buy URL? I can run a scan and give you a much more specific answer to all of these against your actual store.
>
> — Mayank

---

### 9. Aggressive / suspicious / "is this legit?"

**Shape**: Don't get defensive. Show, don't argue.

> Totally fair to ask.
>
> I'm Mayank — solo dev based in [location]. Building this in public on @mayankmalikx where you can see the actual work. Migrations are output-only (CSV file, no access to your store, no logins needed from you). I send a report with every output so you can verify everything before importing anywhere.
>
> Happy to do a free scan first so you see what you'd actually get.
>
> — Mayank

---

### 10. Agency / "do you do bulk?"

**Shape**: Yes interest, but qualify volume + use case.

> Yes — agencies are the second audience for this.
>
> What's the volume look like? If you've got 5+ stores to migrate, I can do them all at the same price point as a single, since the bottleneck is per-store not per-batch.
>
> — Mayank

---

### 11. Hindi / Hinglish DM

**Shape**: Reply in their mix. Don't switch to formal English on them.

> Hey [name],
>
> Haan, dm2buy se Shopify migrate kar sakte hain. Tum apna store URL bhej do — main free scan kar dunga, fir batata hoon kitne products aur kya kya milega.
>
> — Mayank

(Mayank should tone-match — if they wrote formal Hindi, reply formal. If casual Hinglish, casual back.)

---

### 12. Polite no / not the right time

**Shape**: Don't push. Leave the door open.

> No worries — keep the link handy if it ever comes up.
>
> If you know someone on dm2buy who's outgrown it, sending them my way is helpful.
>
> — Mayank

---

## What NEVER to write in a DM

- "Hi! Thanks for reaching out! 😊"
- "Our team will get back to you within 24-48 business hours"
- "We're excited to assist you!"
- Any sentence starting with "We believe"
- Anything that implies a team when it's just Mayank
- More than one question per reply (overwhelming)
- Links to multiple things — pick one

---

## Response time targets

- @mayankmalikx personal DMs → within a few hours, same day max
- @shoprift DMs → within 24h
- Email → within 24h

When responding late: skip the apology. Just answer the question. "Hey — answering this now:" is plenty.
