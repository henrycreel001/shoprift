# Shoprift Voice Rules — Full Reference

Source: `shoprift-voice-messaging.html` and `shoprift-brand-guidelines.html` in the Shoprift project.

---

## The Four Pillars

Every sentence has to pass all four. If it doesn't, rewrite.

### 1. Direct
Lead with the answer. Cut the warmup. Short sentences. Concrete numbers. Zero corporate softeners.

> ✓ "100 products. 3 minutes. Done."
> ✗ "Blazing-fast performance optimized for modern e-commerce."

### 2. Technical
Use the real words. CSV, SKU, listings, collections, import. Don't dumb it down. The audience already runs a store.

> ✓ "Outputs a Shopify-ready product CSV with image URLs preserved."
> ✗ "A user-friendly export of your store data."

### 3. Understated
No "revolutionary." No "game-changing." The product is impressive enough that hyperbole cheapens it. State facts; let the user be impressed on their own.

> ✓ "Migrate your store. That's the whole thing."
> ✗ "Experience the future of e-commerce migration."

### 4. Human
Built by one person. Show that. First person on personal channels. Founder available in DMs. Tool-grade product, founder-grade support.

> ✓ "Built by one person. DM me on X if it breaks."
> ✗ "Our dedicated support team is here to assist you 24/7."

## Word Bank

### USE (the good list)
**Verbs**: migrate, port, move, ship, drop in, extract, export, import, push, pull, hit, send

**Nouns**: rift, store, products, listings, collections, images, SKUs, CSV, URL, dashboard, dm2buy, Shopify

**Adjectives** (use sparingly): every, exact, full, complete, clean, ready, done

**Time/scale words**: minutes, seconds, hours, three, ten, hundred, all, every

### AVOID (the bad list)
**Banned outright**:
- revolutionary
- seamless
- empower / empowerment
- unleash
- leverage
- cutting-edge
- game-changing / game changer
- best-in-class
- robust
- synergy
- ecosystem
- journey
- solution (in product sense)
- dedicated (as in "dedicated team")
- world-class
- next-generation / next-gen
- innovative
- holistic
- end-to-end
- turnkey
- frictionless
- delight (as a verb)

**Marketing-coded softeners**: streamline, optimize, transform (in the marketing sense — fine as a literal verb), elevate, supercharge, accelerate (unless literal), reimagine, redefine

**Phrases to never write**:
- "In today's fast-paced world…"
- "We believe that…"
- "Our mission is to…"
- "Join us as we…"
- "Game-changing solution"
- "Best-in-class platform"
- "Trusted by industry leaders"
- "Take your business to the next level"

## Punctuation & Capitalization

- **No exclamation marks. Anywhere. Ever.**
- Periods only. The voice is calm. Excitement is communicated through specificity (numbers, claims), not punctuation.
- **Sentence case** is the default everywhere.
- **Lowercase** is permitted only on: Mayank's personal Instagram (@mayankmalikx), X/Twitter posts. Not on @shoprift, not in YouTube descriptions, not in DMs.
- Em dashes are fine. So are colons. Don't ladder five clauses.
- **No emoji** except: ✓ (success), → (forward arrow / CTA), ↓ (link below). That's the entire allowed set.
- "dm2buy" is lowercase, always. "Shopify" is capitalized normally.
- "Shoprift" is capitalized in sentences. "shoprift" is the wordmark used in the logo lockup and bios — that's its only lowercase use.

## Stylistic moves (use sparingly — each once per piece max)

- **The triple**: "Every product. Every image. Every collection."
- **The closer**: "That's the whole thing."
- **The number drop**: "100 products in 3 minutes."
- **The shrug**: "It just works." / "No setup. No code."
- **The human escape hatch**: "Stuck? DM me." / "Built by one person."

If you use more than one of these in a single short piece (carousel, post), the voice starts to feel like a tic. Vary.

## Tone calibration: how harsh is too harsh?

The voice is **understated**, not aggressive. The brand doesn't shit-talk dm2buy. It just shows what Shopify (or any better platform) offers.

- ✓ "When you outgrow dm2buy, the move shouldn't take weeks."
- ✗ "dm2buy is a broken platform that's holding you back."

Punching down is off-brand. The voice is confident enough not to need it.

## The pitch-shape library

Standard ways to frame Shoprift, all on-brand. Mix and match.

- **One-liner**: "Move your dm2buy store to Shopify in minutes. Every product, every image, every collection."
- **Mechanism**: "Drop in your dm2buy URL. Get a CSV ready to import."
- **Time framing**: "Minutes, not days."
- **Scope framing**: "Every product. Every image. Every collection."
- **Founder framing**: "Built by one person. For sellers who outgrew dm2buy."
- **Platform framing**: "dm2buy → Shopify. Or WooCommerce. Or any platform that takes a CSV."

## The four-question gut check (run before delivering)

1. Did I lead with the answer?
2. Could a senior engineer read this without cringing?
3. Is there a concrete number/word doing real work?
4. Does this sound like one person wrote it, not a marketing team?

If any answer is no, rewrite.
