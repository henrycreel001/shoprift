# Channel Playbook

How each channel works. Voice + format rules per platform.

---

## @shoprift (Instagram) — product brand

**Bio**:
```
shoprift
The rift between storefronts.
Move your dm2buy store to Shopify in minutes.
↓ shoprift.io
```

**Voice**: Product brand. Sentence case. First-person plural ("we built…") or third-person product voice ("Shoprift moves…"). Direct, technical, understated.

**Default formats**:
- **Carousels** (the workhorse) — 5-10 slides, HTML-rendered, screenshot each, post as carousel
- **Single posts** — one strong visual, short caption
- **Reels** — product demo / explainer, no face needed (screen recordings work great)
- **Stories** — quick updates, polls, behind-the-scenes (links to recent posts)

**Caption shape**:
1. Hook line (one sentence)
2. The detail (2-3 short lines)
3. CTA — one only. Either "DM us" or "Link in bio."

**Hashtags**: 3-5 max, all relevant. `#dm2buy #shopify #d2cindia #indianecommerce #ecommercemigration`. Never use #love #instagood etc.

## @mayankmalikx (Instagram) — founder building in public

**Voice**: First person. Lowercase OK. Show the work — screenshots, code, real numbers, real wins, real bugs, real DMs (blurred names).

**What to post**:
- "shipped X today" updates
- screenshots of code, terminal output, supabase tables, designs
- numbers — DM count, sellers helped, products migrated, lines of code
- bugs and how they got fixed (the bug confession is brand-positive)
- threads about what migration is teaching you about indian d2c
- before/after of manual migration work you did

**What NOT to post**:
- Generic motivational stuff. Nobody asked.
- Personal life that has nothing to do with the build. Wrong account for it.
- Predictions. "Indian d2c will be $X by 2030." Boring and probably wrong.

**Caption shape**:
- Open with the action: "shipped X." / "fixed Y today." / "just helped a seller migrate Z products."
- A line of detail. Maybe a screenshot.
- Optional founder reflection — but max 2 sentences. Not a sermon.
- Soft CTA at the end is OK: "DM if you're on dm2buy and want to see this when it's ready."

## Reels — both accounts

**Structure** (this is the spec for reel scripts):

```
HOOK (0-3 sec) — the first line on screen + first words spoken
  - Pattern: a number, a confession, or a counter-intuitive claim
  - "I migrated 213 products in 4 minutes."
  - "dm2buy doesn't have a CSV export. So I built one."
  - "Your dm2buy store can move to Shopify. Here's how."

SCRIPT (15-60 sec spoken)
  - 1 idea. Not 5.
  - Short sentences. Pause-friendly.
  - End with a beat (something to ground the close)

ON-SCREEN TEXT
  - Reinforces, doesn't repeat. Numbers, key terms, the "what" while you're explaining the "why."
  - Sans-serif. White on dark. Brand-accent green for the punchline.

B-ROLL / VISUALS
  - Screen recording of the actual tool / process if possible
  - Cuts of the dashboard, CSV downloading, products appearing in Shopify admin
  - For face reels: keep camera at eye level, soft front light, plain background

CTA
  - One. "DM me 'migrate' for early access." Or "Link in bio."
  - Never "follow for more." It's begging. Show value; follows come.

CAPTION (under the reel)
  - 1-2 sentences max. Same voice rules.
```

## YouTube — explainer videos (Mayank-on-camera)

**Format**: See `templates/youtube-script.md` for the full script template.

**Target length**: 2-4 minutes for explainers. 5-8 for deeper dives.

**Voice on camera**: Mayank's voice — direct, technical, slightly tired-of-marketing-fluff. First person. Don't perform.

**Title rules**:
- No clickbait. No "YOU WON'T BELIEVE…"
- Direct titles work: "How to move your dm2buy store to Shopify" / "What's in a Shoprift migration CSV"
- Numbers are good: "I migrated 213 products in 4 minutes. Here's how."
- Avoid emoji in titles entirely.

**Thumbnail rules**:
- Brand: Void background (#0A0B0F), Rift Mint accent (#00E5A0), Geist or JetBrains Mono type
- Or: face + 3-word overlay + one product / dashboard screenshot
- No arrow-pointing-at-face thumbnails. No mouth-open shocked-face thumbnails.

**Description**:
- First sentence repeats the value prop
- Link to landing page
- Mayank's IG + email at the bottom

## Reddit

**The cardinal rule**: Be native. If you sound like a marketer, you're downvoted into oblivion within 2 hours.

**Voice**: Match the sub. Most ecom/startup subs respond best to:
- First person ("I built a tool that…")
- A real story / a real number
- A genuine question or learning shared
- Optional, soft link to Shoprift at the end — but only if it adds value, not as the lede

**Post shapes that work**:
- **The build log**: "I'm building a dm2buy → Shopify migration tool. Here's what I've learned from talking to 50 sellers."
- **The lesson share**: "Migrated 12 dm2buy stores manually last month. Here are the 5 things that always break."
- **The genuine question**: "Has anyone here moved a store from dm2buy to Shopify? Curious how you handled X."

**Post shapes that DIE**:
- "Check out my new tool! 🚀" (every word wrong)
- Anything that reads like a press release
- "Hey r/[sub], thought you'd find this useful…" then a thinly-veiled ad

**Always do**:
- Read the sub's rules before posting. Most have anti-self-promo rules.
- Check the sub's recent top posts to match tone.
- Reply to every comment. Reddit rewards engagement.
- If a mod removes the post, don't argue. Repost elsewhere.

**See `references/reddit-subs.md` for the target sub list.**

## DMs & enquiries

**Voice**: Founder, warm, short. Always sign "— Mayank" if responding personally.

**Rules**:
- Match their energy. If they wrote 3 lines, write 3 lines.
- Answer the question first. Pleasantries after, if at all.
- Hindi-English mix in inbound? Reply in their mix.
- Never auto-respond with templates that feel templated. Personalize the first line.
- "DM me 'migrate'" inbound → respond within 24 hours, ideally same day.
- If pricing is asked and pricing isn't public yet: "Custom quote after I see the store. Send the URL?" — no apologies, no over-explanation.

**See `templates/dm-enquiry-templates.md` for common scenarios.**

## Comments on others' content

**Where to comment**:
- Posts by D2C founders on Instagram
- dm2buy seller posts (about their stores, their struggles)
- Shopify-related threads
- Indian startup / e-commerce Twitter
- YouTube videos about D2C / Shopify / migration

**What works**:
- A specific observation that shows you read the post
- A relevant resource / experience (yours, not Shoprift's)
- A question that adds to the discussion

**What doesn't**:
- "Great post 🚀"
- "Check out shoprift.io"
- "DM me to learn more"
- Anything that mentions Shoprift unprompted in the first sentence

**The rule**: Comment 9 times helpfully. The 10th time, *if relevant*, mention what you're building. Never the other way around.
