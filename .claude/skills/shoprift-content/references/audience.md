# Shoprift Audience

## Primary: dm2buy graduates

**Who they are**:
- Indian D2C sellers running fashion, handmade, crochet, jewelry, candles, or similar small-batch brands on Instagram + dm2buy
- Have scaled past dm2buy's ceiling — typically **100+ SKUs, ₹5L+/month** in revenue
- Started on dm2buy because it was the easiest way to bolt a checkout onto Instagram
- Now hitting limits: variants, inventory management, custom themes, international payments, shipping integrations, third-party apps, abandoned cart recovery
- Ready for Shopify — but the migration is what's blocking them

**What they care about**:
- Not losing product data (descriptions, prices, images, variants, collections)
- Not having to re-do months of work
- Time — most are solo or two-person operations, no agency to throw money at
- Trust — they want to know who built it before they hand over their store URL
- Cost — they're not paying ₹50k to a migration agency, but they'll pay for something fair

**What they don't care about**:
- Buzzwords. They've seen them. They tune out.
- "Innovation." They want the boring thing that works.
- Long demos. They want a 30-second answer to "what does it do."

**How they talk**:
- Practical. "How long does it take?" "What if my images don't transfer?" "Can I see what comes out?"
- Specific. "I have 247 products and 12 collections."
- Hindi-English mix is common in DMs. Content stays English unless they switch first.

---

## Secondary: agencies & freelancers

**Who they are**: Solo freelancers, small agencies, ex-employees of D2C brands. They do migrations as a side service for clients.

**What they care about**:
- Speed — a 30-minute job vs a 3-day job changes their margins
- Reliability — if one product is missing, their client is angry
- Being able to do this for clients without sharing credentials

**Why they matter**: They're force multipliers. One agency = 20 stores.

---

## Future: any-platform-to-any-platform sellers

Not the focus today. Mentioned in copy only when discussing where Shoprift goes long-term. The Brand Essence document calls this the future vision: "the default migration layer for e-commerce platforms."

---

## What this means for content

1. **Talk like the audience talks.** Specific. Practical. No jargon they didn't sign up for.
2. **Show, don't promise.** Screenshots, real product counts, before/after CSV samples. Not "trust us, it works."
3. **Address the actual fears**:
   - "Will I lose product descriptions?" → No. Every field carries over.
   - "Will images break?" → Images get downloaded and re-uploaded, not hotlinked.
   - "What if I have variants?" → Variants carry over as Shopify variants.
   - "How do I know it worked?" → You get a migration report. Every product accounted for.
4. **Never patronize.** They know what a CSV is. They know what a SKU is. Don't explain those.
5. **Match the format to the audience's medium.** They live on Instagram. Carousels > long blogs. Reels > webinars.

---

## The audience question to ask before writing

> If a dm2buy seller with 200 SKUs and ₹8L/month scrolled past this in their feed, would they stop? Would they DM? Or would they keep scrolling?

If the answer is "keep scrolling," it's too generic. Add a number, a specific fear, a real screenshot, or kill the post.
